About Me

My name is Gyula (Jules) — I'm a European multilingual engineer who's lived across Europe, Southeast Asia, New Zealand, and Australia before landing in Boston two years ago to pursue a Master's in Computer Science at Northeastern University.

Before pivoting to CS, I spent three years in finance — first as a Treasury Analyst at Morgan Stanley, then as a Fixed Income Reference Data Analyst at MSCI. That experience gave me a deep appreciation for reliable systems, clean data, and what happens when production breaks at 2am.

Now I build things. My work spans distributed systems (MapReduce in Go, deployed on AWS ECS), machine learning (hybrid search reranking with BERT, reinforcement learning for autonomous driving), and full-stack web apps. I'm most at home somewhere between the infrastructure ,the model and low level code — where code meets reality.

Outside of coding, I'm into tea culture, 3D printing, laser cutting, and tinkering with Arduino and ESP32 projects. I run a self-hosted Raspberry Pi NAS at home and currently trying to automate my daily tasks with agentic AI.

I'm currently looking for software engineering roles where I can bring both my technical skills and my cross-domain perspective to a team building something meaningful.
